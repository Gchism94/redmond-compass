Annotations

Numbered to the screen. Tags show what renders when.

  1. Business header ALL

    Name, tier badge, verification, and “view public profile” (see what residents see). Badge color/text tracks the tier. Unverified state replaces this with a verify CTA and locks actions.

  2. Profile completeness ALL

    The best free-tier engagement driver — completeness improves discoverability for everyone, so it helps the owner and the directory. More prominent when low; surfaces the single next best action.

  3. Quick actions ALL

    Post Bulletin · Submit Event · Edit Profile — the mobile-native “standing in my shop” moments. On Free, Post shows the remaining cap (2/3) quietly, not as a countdown of doom.

  4. Value snapshot ALL GATE

    Views/calls/directions/follows are free for every tier — prove value first. The “full analytics” row is the gate: locked → Member on Free, an open link on Member/Pro.

  5. Demand-signal teaser GATE

    The headline hook and the most on-mission upsell. Free sees the shape (“3 searched gluten-free”) with blurred specifics; Member/Pro see the real terms incl. unmet demand. This replaces any ad-style pitch.

  6. Your tools GATE PRO

    Free & Member see a quiet “add tools” card routing to B6 (Pro). Pro turns it into a live strip — booking requests, unread inquiries (incl. private feedback), loyalty activity — each deep-linking to its manage view.

  7. Membership card ALL

    Contextual and singular. Free pitches Member (with a Pro nod); Member nudges Pro for tools + manage; Pro goes quiet to “manage plan · cancel.” Arriving from a specific limit, this leads with that benefit. One card, never a wall of upsells.

  8. Switch to consumer view ALL

    Top-right — owners are also residents. Mode switch lives here and in Account (S8). Other states: incomplete (meter dominates), unverified (actions locked), offline (cached snapshot).

Open questions. Whether the demand teaser blurs specifics or just hides counts · how hard the Member→Pro nudge should push (risk of nagging existing payers) · whether completeness should weight discoverability visibly (“profiles at 100% get 3× the calls”) · default snapshot window (7 vs 30 days). Next: consumer-side surfaces — the S5 Recommend action + the S7 Rewards wallet — or the S4/S3 search pair that reuses the S5 action vocabulary.
