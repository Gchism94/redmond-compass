# What's Happening in Redmond

Stay informed on news that directly impacts your community

### The Fourth That Kept Firefighters Busy

A full accounting of Fourth of July weekend fires across Central Oregon, from Bend arrests to Redmond's vacant house fire and the Coyner Fire.

### Redmond Compass Roundup: July 6, 2026

Fourth of July weekend brought parades, parties, and several fires to Central Oregon. Here is what happened around Redmond, Terrebonne, Crooked River Ranch, and the wider region.

### Coyner Fire Prompts Evacuations in NW Redmond Over Holiday Weekend

A wildfire broke out Saturday evening in northwest Redmond, sending one neighborhood into a Level 3 'Go Now' evacuation and a wider area into a Level 2 'Get Set' warning. The Coyner Fire grew to about 11 acres before crews stopped its forward progress.

### A Loss at Blue Pool, and a Reminder for Everyone Who Loves It

A 21-year-old student drowned at Tamolitch Blue Pool on June 26. The pool's 37-degree water and cliff jumping risks make it one of Oregon's most dangerous swimming holes.

### Coyner Fire Prompts Level 3 Evacuations Near Redmond

Fire burned within feet of homes northwest of Redmond over the holiday weekend, triggering a Level 3 'Go Now' evacuation. No homes were lost.

### Chlorine Leak Closes Centennial Park

Downtown Redmond's splash park closed Monday, July 6 after a chlorine leak. Crews waited on the HazMat team from Salem; no injuries reported.

### Two Bend Men Arrested After Roman Candle Fire

Men allegedly shot Roman candles at each other July 4, sparking a fire near a business with propane tanks. Bend police logged 85 firework complaint emails that week.

### Fire Heavily Damages Two Homes on NW Greenwood Avenue

A fire on Saturday afternoon heavily damaged two homes in the 2700 block of NW Greenwood Avenue. One home was unoccupied and listed for sale, while a resident in the second home escaped safely after a passing neighbor alerted her to the flames.

### Redmond's Fourth of July Parade Brings Crowds Downtown

Redmond's Independence Day parade brought crowds downtown Saturday morning for the community's long running holiday tradition, a nice reminder of how many neighbors and local businesses turn out to support it each year.

### Road Work and Closures to Plan Around This Week

The City of Redmond's weekly traffic report lists several closures residents and business owners should plan around, including a crane operation on SW 9th Street, continued closures on NW Birch Avenue and NW 8th Street, and a street closure on NW Elm Avenue and NW 29th Street from July 10 through July 17.

### District-Wide Burn Ban Remains in Effect Amid Holiday Fire Activity

With two fires burning in Redmond over the same holiday weekend, it's a good moment to note that Redmond Fire and Rescue's district-wide burn ban remains active. Residents and business owners are asked to hold off on debris, weeds, or brush fires and report any smoke or flames promptly to 911.
