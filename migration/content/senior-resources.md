# Support for Older Adults and Their Families

Getting older sometimes means needing a little extra help, whether that’s a hot meal, a ride, someone to talk to, or help sorting out Medicare. You don’t have to figure it out alone. The organizations below serve Redmond and the wider Central Oregon region and most of their help is free.

## Council on Aging of Central Oregon

The Council on Aging is the regional hub for aging services across Bend, Redmond, Sisters, La Pine, Madras, and Prineville. Their Help Desk can point you toward Meals on Wheels, Medicare counseling, caregiver support, in-home help, and more, one call gets you started.

## Redmond Senior Center

The local hub for Redmond and Crooked River Ranch seniors. Delivers Meals on Wheels, runs community dining and social activities, and offers Passion for Pets, a program that provides pet food to homebound Meals on Wheels clients so they can keep caring for their animals.

## Quick resource links

### Medicare Counseling (SHIBA)

Free, unbiased help understanding Medicare plans and choices from trained volunteer counselors.

### Aging and Disability Resource Connection (ADRC)

Not sure where to start? This statewide line connects you to local aging and disability services and support.

### Family Caregiver Support

Guidance, respite options, and support for people caring for an aging loved one.

### Options Counseling

Free help weighing choices for staying at home safely versus other levels of care.

## Directions: The Central Oregon Guide for Older Adults

A free, comprehensive directory covering supportive services, emergency contacts, financial and legal help, and social opportunities for older adults across the region.

Council on Aging also partners with the Latino Community Association for bilingual support, see our New to the Area page for details.
