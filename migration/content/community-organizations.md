# Community Organizations & Service Clubs

Service clubs, veterans organizations, fraternal lodges, arts groups, and civic organizations that bring our community together across Redmond, Terrebonne, and Crooked River Ranch.

## Service Clubs

Fellowship, service, and community

### Rotary Club of Redmond

Meets weekly for lunch, a guest speaker, and community service projects. Redmond Rotary supports local youth, veterans, and community initiatives, and is part of Rotary International District 5110.

Thursdays, noon to 1 pm.

### Kiwanis Club of Redmond

One of the longest-running service clubs in Deschutes County, serving the community since 1937. Kiwanis focuses on children and youth. Meets weekly for lunch.

Weekly, noon to 1 pm.

### Redmond Lions Club

Part of Lions Clubs International, one of the largest service organizations in the world. The Redmond chapter focuses on vision, community service, and local youth.

Second Tuesday at noon and fourth Tuesday at 6 pm.

### Crooked River Ranch Lions Club

The Lions chapter serving Crooked River Ranch and the surrounding Terrebonne area. Meets twice monthly for dinner and a program.

Second and fourth Tuesdays. Social at 5:30 pm, meeting at 6:30 pm.

### Toastmasters of Redmond

A free weekly meeting for anyone who wants to build public speaking and leadership skills in a supportive, friendly environment. Open to all.

Weekly, noon to 1 pm.

## Veterans Organizations

Honoring those who served

### VFW Post 4108 and Ladies Auxiliary (Deschutes Post)

Veterans of Foreign Wars post serving Redmond and the surrounding area. Offers a social hall, veteran support, community events, and scholarships. The Ladies Auxiliary meets the first Thursday of the month at 6 pm.

### American Legion Ray Johnson Post 44

Redmond's American Legion post, serving veterans and the community. Hosts public bingo (Wednesdays and Fridays at 6 pm, Sundays at 12:30 pm). Open to the public for bingo events.

### Military Order of the Purple Heart

Serves Purple Heart recipients and veterans in the Redmond area. Contact through VFW Post 4108.

## Fraternal and Civic Lodges

Tradition, fellowship, and community service

### Moose Lodge 323 and Women of the Moose

A social and charitable fraternal organization open to men and women. Supports Mooseheart and Moosehaven, as well as local community causes.

### Redmond Masonic Lodge 154

One of the oldest civic organizations in Redmond, with over 100 years of history in the community. Regular meetings on the second Monday of each month at 7 pm (except July and August), with dinner at 6 pm. Dining room and kitchen available for community rental (seats up to 80).

Second Monday of the month, 7 pm (dinner at 6 pm). Closed July and August.

### Redmond Rebekah Lodge 204

A fraternal organization with deep roots in Redmond, focused on charity, truth, and universal brotherhood and sisterhood.

## Arts and Culture

Creativity and community

### Dry Canyon Arts Association (DCAA)

A nonprofit 501(c)(3) arts organization founded in Redmond to promote, support, and expand access to visual and performing arts across Jefferson, Deschutes, and Crook Counties. Working toward a dedicated community arts facility in Redmond. Member and board meetings on the fourth Thursday of each month at 5:30 pm. Open to artists and non-artists alike.

Fourth Thursday of the month, 5:30 pm.

## Civic and Community Organizations

Building a better community

### Bend-Redmond Habitat for Humanity

Builds and repairs affordable homes for families in Deschutes County. Volunteers are welcome, no construction experience required. Accepts donations of new and gently used building materials, appliances, and home goods at the ReStore.

### Friends of the Redmond Library

A volunteer group that supports the Deschutes Public Library Redmond Branch through fundraising, programming support, and community outreach.

### General Federation of Women's Clubs of Central Oregon (GFWC)

Part of the oldest and largest nonpartisan women's volunteer service organization in the world. The Central Oregon chapter focuses on community service, education, and civic engagement.

### American Association of University Women (AAUW), Redmond Branch

Advances gender equity through advocacy, education, and research. Open to anyone who holds an associate's degree or higher. The Redmond branch hosts programs and events throughout the year.

### Redmond Flag Committee

A community group dedicated to maintaining Redmond's Flag City USA designation and promoting patriotic displays throughout the city.

### Redmond Grange

Part of the National Grange, a civic organization with agricultural roots and a community focus. The Redmond Grange hall is used for community events and club meetings.

## Dance and Social Clubs

Get moving and get connected

### Redrock Squares

A square dance club meeting twice monthly. Open to the public. Beginners welcome.

First and third Fridays, 7 to 9:30 pm. $8 per dancer.

### Sundown Ridge Riders Round Dance Club

A round dance club meeting twice monthly in Redmond. Open to all.

First and third Thursdays, 6:30 to 9 pm. $7 per dancer.
